What is Stranger Things?

"Stranger Things" is a popular Netflix sci-fi horror series created by the Duffer Brothers. Set in the 1980s, the show takes place in the fictional town of Hawkins, Indiana, where a young boy, Will Byers, mysteriously disappears. His friends, along with a telekinetic girl named Eleven, discover that his disappearance is linked to a parallel dimension called the Upside Down, which is filled with dangerous creatures. The series is known for its nostalgic references to 1980s pop culture.

Throughout its seasons, Stranger Things explores government conspiracies, secret experiments, and the impact of the Upside Down on the town of Hawkins. The ensemble cast, including Winona Ryder, David Harbour, Millie Bobby Brown, and Finn Wolfhard, has received praise for their performances.
